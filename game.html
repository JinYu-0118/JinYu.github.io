Generation I

Pokémon Red and Green

Released in 1996 (Japan). These were the first Pokémon games
that introduced players to the world of Pokémon,
starting the journey in the Kanto region with 151 Pokémon.

Pokémon Blue

Released in 1996 (Japan). Initially available only through a special mail-order offer,
Pokémon Blue featured updated graphics and fixed bugs present in Red and Green.
The international release combined elements of Blue with the original games.

Pokémon Yellow

Released in 1998. An enhanced version of Red, Green and Blue,
this game followed the storyline of the anime,
allowing players to start with Pikachu as their partner.

Pokémon Fire Red and Leaf Green

Released in 2004. Remakes of the original Red and Green games,
these versions brought the classic Kanto adventure to the
Game Boy Advance with updated graphics, sound and
additional features like the Sevii Islands.

Generation II

Pokémon Gold and Silver

Released in 1999. These games introduced
the Johto region and 100 new Pokémon,
along with new features like the day-night cycle and breeding.

Pokémon Crystal

Released in 2000. The first Pokémon game to feature
animated sprites and allow players to choose
between a male or female protagonist.

Pokémon Heart Gold and Soul Silver

Released in 2009. Remakes of Gold and Silver,
these games brought the Johto adventure to the Nintendo DS,
featuring updated graphics, touchscreen features and
the Pokéwalker, a pedometer accessory that allowed
players to transfer Pokémon and earn in-game rewards.

Generation III

Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire

Released in 2002. Set in the Hoenn region,
these games introduced double battles and abilities,
adding new strategic elements to gameplay.

Pokémon Emerald

Released in 2004. An enhanced version of
Ruby and Sapphire, featuring the Battle Frontier,
a challenging post-game area with various battle facilities.

Pokémon Omega Ruby and Alpha Sapphire

Released in 2014. Remakes of the Generation III games
with updated graphics, new features, and an expanded story.

Generation IV

Pokémon Diamond and Pearl

Released in 2006. Introducing the Sinnoh region and
107 new Pokémon, these games brought online trading and
battling via the Nintendo Wi-Fi Connection.

Pokémon Platinum

Released in 2008. An enhanced version of Diamond and Pearl,
featuring the Distortion World and a more prominent role for Giratina.

Pokémon Brilliant Diamond and Shining Pearl

Released in 2021. Faithful remakes of the original Diamond and Pearl games
with updated graphics and modernized gameplay features.

Generation V

Pokémon Black and White

Released in 2010. Set in the Unova region,
these games introduced 156 new Pokémon,
the most of any generation and focused on
a more story-driven narrative.

Pokémon Black 2 and White 2

Released in 2012. Direct sequels to Black and White,
these games continued the story in Unova,
adding new locations and characters.

Generation VI

Pokémon X and Y

Released in 2013. Set in the Kalos region, these were
the first main series games to feature fully 3D graphics and
introduced Mega Evolution, a temporary transformation that
boosts a Pokémon's stats.

Generation VII

Pokémon Sun and Moon

Released in 2016. Set in the Alola region, these games
introduced Alolan forms of existing Pokémon, Z-Moves and
removed traditional gyms in favor of Island Challenges

Pokémon Ultra Sun and Ultra Moon

Released in 2017. Enhanced versions of Sun and Moon
with additional story elements and new features,
such as the Ultra Warp Ride.

Generation VIII

Pokémon Sword and Shield

Released in 2019. Set in the Galar region,
these games introduced Dynamax and Gigantamax forms,
the Wild Area, an open-world environment with roaming Pokémon.

Pokémon Legends: Arceus

Released in 2022. A groundbreaking entry that
blends traditional Pokémon gameplay
with open-world exploration and a focus on
capturing Pokémon in real-time.

Generation IX

Pokémon Scarlet and Violet

Released in 2022. These are the first open-world Pokémon RPGs
set in the Paldea region, introducing the Terastal phenomenon and
offering a non-linear gameplay experience. Players can explore
the world freely while engaging in Pokémon battles and challenges.
